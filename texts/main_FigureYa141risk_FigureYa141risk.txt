You can also use X-tile to find the best grouping cutoff; Or use FigureYa4bestSeparation to find the grouping with the smallest P value; Or use FigureYa35batch_bestSeparation to find the best grouping from a group of genes in batches, and screen out genes with significant differences between the two groups. reorder_cormat <- function(cormat){
  dd <- dist(cormat)
  hc <- hclust(dd,method = "average")
  cormat <-cormat[hc$order,]
}
tmp1 <- reorder_cormat(tmp)
tmp1 <- rbind(tmp1,ifelse(rs_data$Risk=="Low-risk",-1.5,1.5))
